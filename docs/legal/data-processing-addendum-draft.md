# Data Processing Addendum Draft

Last Updated: March 29, 2026

This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") supplements the agreement between [Company Legal Name], doing business as AI Content Risk Score ("Provider"), and the customer entity that has entered into an agreement for the Services ("Customer").

This DPA is intended for business customers and enterprise workspaces. It should be incorporated into the main terms or an order form where Customer requests data-processing terms.

## 1. Roles of the Parties

For Customer Content and personal data submitted by or on behalf of Customer through the Services:

- Customer acts as controller or business, as applicable.
- Provider acts as processor, service provider, or contractor, as applicable, when processing that data on Customer's behalf to provide the Services.

The parties acknowledge that certain third parties may act as independent controllers for their own services, especially payment processors used directly by Customer or end users.

## 2. Subject Matter, Duration, and Purpose

### Subject matter

Provider processes personal data contained in Customer Content and related account/workspace data for the purpose of providing the Services.

### Duration

Processing continues for the duration of the applicable services relationship and any permitted post-termination retention period required for security, backup, compliance, or dispute resolution.

### Nature and purpose

Processing may include:

- hosting and storing uploaded assets and related metadata;
- authenticating users and administering workspaces;
- analyzing Customer Content for IP risk, brand safety, provenance, and mitigation workflows;
- generating reports and outputs;
- maintaining logs, audit trails, and security events;
- supporting purchases, subscriptions, receipts, and account administration; and
- providing customer support and incident response.

## 3. Categories of Data and Data Subjects

### Data subjects

- Customer personnel and workspace users
- Customer end users or contractors uploaded into workspace records
- individuals appearing in uploaded media
- support and billing contacts

### Categories of personal data

- name, email address, company, role, and account identifiers
- workspace membership and administrative data
- uploaded content, including images, videos, filenames, and embedded metadata
- brand guidelines, policy documents, and text submitted for analysis
- scan results, findings, provenance metadata, and mitigation outputs
- usage, device, browser, IP-derived, session, and analytics data
- billing identifiers and transaction metadata

Provider does not intentionally require Customer to submit special-category or sensitive data, but Customer Content may incidentally contain sensitive or regulated information. Customer is responsible for determining whether use of the Services is appropriate for such information.

## 4. Provider Obligations

Provider will:

- process personal data only on documented instructions from Customer, unless required by law;
- ensure persons authorized to process personal data are bound by confidentiality obligations;
- implement appropriate technical and organizational measures designed to protect personal data;
- assist Customer, taking into account the nature of processing, with reasonable requests concerning data-subject rights, security incidents, impact assessments, and regulatory inquiries;
- notify Customer of a confirmed personal-data breach affecting Customer data without undue delay, consistent with applicable law and Provider incident-response procedures; and
- delete or return Customer personal data at the end of the services relationship, except to the extent retention is required by law or reasonably necessary for backup, security, fraud prevention, dispute resolution, or contract enforcement.

Absent Customer's separate express written permission, Provider will not use Customer personal data submitted through the Services to train Provider's own machine-learning models.

## 5. Customer Obligations

Customer is responsible for:

- ensuring it has a valid legal basis for submitting personal data to the Services;
- providing required notices to data subjects;
- obtaining required consents or permissions;
- configuring the Services in a lawful manner;
- managing workspace users and permissions;
- evaluating whether the Services are suitable for sensitive or regulated content; and
- reviewing and acting on outputs with appropriate human oversight.

Customer authorizes Provider to use subprocessors in connection with the Services. As of this draft, expected subprocessors or service providers include:

| Provider | Function |

Optional enterprise step:

- publish a standalone subprocessor page and add notice/objection mechanics for enterprise customers.

## 7. International Transfers

Where personal data subject to EEA, UK, or similar transfer restrictions is transferred internationally, the parties will rely on a lawful transfer mechanism such as standard contractual clauses, adequacy decisions, or another recognized mechanism.

## 8. Security Measures

Provider will maintain commercially reasonable safeguards designed for the Services, which may include:

- access controls and role-based permissions;
- tenant-scoped data segregation and authorization checks;
- encryption in transit;
- vendor-managed infrastructure security controls;
- logging, monitoring, and rate limiting;
- change management and least-privilege practices; and
- incident-response procedures.

Provider does not guarantee that the Services are immune from all security incidents.

## 9. Audits and Information Requests

Upon reasonable written request, Provider will make available information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA. Any audit rights beyond documentary responses should be limited to enterprise customers, subject to confidentiality, advance notice, reasonable scope, frequency limits, and protection of other customers and systems.

## 10. Return and Deletion

Upon termination or expiration of the services relationship, Provider will delete or return Customer personal data in accordance with the underlying agreement and Provider's retention practices, unless retention is required by law or reasonably necessary for backup, legal, accounting, anti-fraud, or security purposes.

## 11. Order of Precedence

If there is a conflict between this DPA and the main agreement, this DPA will control only with respect to data-protection subject matter.
