# Cookie Notice Draft

Last Updated: March 29, 2026

This Cookie Notice explains how AI Content Risk Score uses cookies, local storage, pixels, and similar technologies ("Cookies") in connection with the Services.

This notice should be published together with a consent banner or preference center before non-essential analytics technologies are activated where consent is required by law.

## 1. What are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files or similar technologies placed on your browser or device. We also use local storage and similar tools that can remember information across sessions.

## 2. Why We Use Cookies

We use Cookies to:

- keep you signed in and maintain secure sessions;
- associate anonymous uploads and report unlock flows with a browser session;
- remember basic settings and preferences;
- understand product usage and improve performance; and
- detect abuse, prevent fraud, and troubleshoot errors.

## 3. Categories of Cookies We Use

### Strictly Necessary

These Cookies are required for the Services to function and cannot be disabled in our systems.

Examples include:

- authentication and session cookies set through our app and authentication provider;
- `ars_session_id`, an httpOnly session identifier used to associate anonymous scan activity and quota with a browser session;
- a short-lived email helper cookie used after guest email capture, currently `magic_auth_email`;
- security, rate-limiting, and fraud-prevention state; and
- infrastructure/session continuity cookies used by hosting or backend providers.

### Preference and Functional Storage

These technologies remember your settings.

Examples include:

- `rs-theme` local storage, used to remember theme preference; and
- similar product settings we may add over time.

### Analytics

These technologies help us understand how people use the Services so we can improve performance and product experience.

Examples include:

- PostHog analytics cookies and local storage entries, such as identifiers in the `ph_*_posthog` family; and
- related event, pageview, and usage tracking state.

We should only deploy analytics Cookies with consent where required by law.

## 4. Current Product-Specific Technology Table

| Technology | Type | Purpose | Typical Duration |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `ars_session_id` | Essential cookie | Associates anonymous scans, session ownership, and quota with a browser | Up to 7 days |
| `magic_auth_email` | Functional cookie | Supports guest unlock flow and pre-fills follow-up billing/auth steps | Up to 24 hours |
| Supabase auth/session cookies | Essential cookie | Maintains authenticated sessions and refresh state | Session or provider-defined |
| `rs-theme` | Local storage | Stores theme preference | Until changed or cleared |
| `ph_*_posthog` and related PostHog storage | Analytics cookie/local storage | Product analytics, pageviews, feature usage, diagnostics | Vendor-defined |

## 5. Your Choices

You can control Cookies in several ways:

- by using our cookie banner or preference center, once implemented;
- by changing browser settings to block or delete Cookies;
- by clearing local storage in your browser; and
- by disabling analytics Cookies where our tools provide that choice.

If you block essential Cookies, some parts of the Services may not function correctly.

## 6. Do Not Track

Some browsers send "Do Not Track" signals. Because there is no universally accepted standard for responding to those signals, we do not currently respond to them in a standardized way.

We may update this Cookie Notice from time to time. If we do, we will post the revised version and update the "Last Updated" date.
